Validity of the NIH toolbox cognitive battery in a healthy oldest-old 85+ sample [PDF]

Journal of the International Neuropsychological Society (2023)

Nolin SA, Cowart H, Merritt S, McInerney K, Bharadwaj PK, Franchetti MK, Raichlen DA, Jessup CJ, Hishaw GA, Van Etten EJ, Trouard TP, Geldmacher DS, Wadley VG, Porges ES, Woods AJ, Cohen RA, Levin BE, Rundek T, Alexander GE, Visscher KM.

Using the MBAR dataset, this paper examines whether a common cognitive battery, the "NIH toolbox cognitive battery," is valid in the oldest old. This paper finds that the NIH toolbox is overall valid, but executive function measures relate to a range of cognitive constructs -- consistent with age-related dedifferentiation in this cohort.

"Surviving and Thriving": evidence for cortical GABA stabilization in cognitively-intact oldest-old adults [PDF]

Translational Psychiatry (2025)

Britton MK, Jensen G, Edden RA, Puts NA, Nolin SA, Merritt SS, Rezaei RF, Forbes M, Johnson KJ, Bharadwaj PK, Franchetti MK, Raichlen DA, Jessup CJ, Hishaw GA, Van Etten EJ, Gudmundson AT, Murali-Manohar S, Cowart H, Trouard TP, Geldmacher DS, Wadley VG, Alperin N, Levin BE, Rundek T, Visscher KM, Woods AJ, Alexander GE, Cohen RA, Porges EC.

The depletion of cortical GABA concentrations is associated with declining cognitive performance in normative aging. This paper analyses GABA in the highest-performing oldest old population, and contributes to the growing body of research that cognition in older adults may require intact GABAergic function.

Network Segregation Predicts Processing Speed in the Cognitively Healthy Oldest-old [PDF]

eLife (2025)

Nolin SA, Faulkner ME, Stewart P, Fleming L, Merritt S, Rezaei RF, Bharadwaj PK, Franchetti MK, Raichlen DA, Jessup CJ, Edwards L, Hishaw GA, Van Etten EJ, Trouard TP, Geldmacher D, Wadley VG, Alperin N, Porges EC, Woods AJ, Cohen RA, Levin BE, Rundek T, Alexander GE, Visscher KM.

This paper examines the brain's network architecture in older adults to show that the brain's 'segregation' predicts processing speed, independent of any evidence for disease.

Associations between physical exercise type, fluid intelligence, executive function, and processing speed in the oldest-old (85+) [PDF]

Geroscience (2024)

Ho BD, Gullett JM, Anton S, Franchetti MK, Bharadwaj PK, Raichlen DA, Alexander GE, Rundek T, Levin B, Visscher K, Woods AJ, Cohen RA.

This paper examines the association between different types of exercise on cognition, indicating that there may be a synergistic effect of aerobic exercise and strength training on executive function.

Larger and more dentated hippocampal structure is associated with better memory in the oldest-old [PDF]

bioRxiv (2022) - Pre-print under review

Parpura V, Caceres HS, Sims SA, Ver Hoef L, Ramaniharan AK, Merritt S, Rezaei RF, Bharadwaj PK, Franchetti MK, Raichlen DA, Jessup CJ, Hishaw AG, Van Etten EJ, Trouard TP, Geldmacher DS, Wadley VG, Alperin N, Porges ES, Woods AJ, Cohen RA, Levin BE, Rundek T, Alexander GE, Visscher KM.

This paper examines how the shape and structure of the hippocampus influences episodic memory, or the ability to recall everyday events, in older adults.
